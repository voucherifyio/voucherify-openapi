<h2 id="stacking-rules">Stacking Rules</h2>
<table>
  <thead>
    <tr>
      <th>Attributes</th>
      <th>Description</th>
    </tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          redeemables_limit
          <br />
          <code>integer</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>Defines how many redeemables can be sent in one request. Note: more redeemables means more processing time.</p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          applicable_redeemables_limit
          <br />
          <code>integer</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>
          Defines how many redeemables can be applied in one request. The number must be less than or equal to
          <code>redeemables_limit</code>.
          For example, a user can select 30 discounts but only 5 will be applied to the order and the remaining will be
          <code>SKIPPED</code>
          according to the
          <code>redeemables_sorting_rule</code>.
        </p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          applicable_redeemables_per_category_limit
          <br />
          <code>integer</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>
          Defines how many redeemables with the same category can be applied in one request. The number must be less than or equal to
          <code>applicable_redeemables_limit</code>.
          The ones above the limit will be
          <code>SKIPPED</code>
          according to the
          <code>redeemables_sorting_rule</code>.
        </p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          applicable_redeemables_category_limits
          <br />
          <code>object</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>Lists categories by category IDs (keys) and defines their limits (values) of applicable redeemables that belong to campaigns with that category.</p>
        <table>
          <thead>
            <tr>
              <th>Attributes</th>
              <th>Description</th>
            </tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td>
                <p>
                  [propertyName]
                  <br />
                  <code>integer</code>
                </p>
              </td>
              <td>
                <p>Limit of applicable redeemables per category.</p>
              </td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          applicable_exclusive_redeemables_limit
          <br />
          <code>integer</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>
          Defines how many redeemables with an assigned exclusive category can be applied in one request. The ones above the limit will be
          <code>SKIPPED</code>
          according to the
          <code>redeemables_sorting_rule</code>.
        </p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          applicable_exclusive_redeemables_per_category_limit
          <br />
          <code>integer</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>
          Defines how many redeemables with an exclusive category per category in stacking rules can be applied in one request. The ones above the limit will be
          <code>SKIPPED</code>
          according to the
          <code>redeemables_sorting_rule</code>.
        </p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          exclusive_categories
          <br />
          <code>array</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>Lists the IDs of exclusive categories. A redeemable from a campaign with an exclusive category is the only redeemable to be redeemed when applied with redeemables from other campaigns unless these campaigns are exclusive or joint.</p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          joint_categories
          <br />
          <code>array</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>Lists the IDs of the joint categories. A campaign with a joint category is always applied regardless of the exclusivity of other campaigns.</p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          redeemables_application_mode
          <br />
          <code>string</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>
          Defines the application mode for redeemables.
          <br />
          <code>"ALL"</code>
          means that all redeemables must be validated for the redemption to be successful.
          <br />
          <code>"PARTIAL"</code>
          means that only those redeemables that can be validated will be redeemed. The redeemables that fail validaton will be skipped.
        </p>
        <p>
          Available values:
          <code>ALL</code>,
          <code>PARTIAL</code>
        </p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          redeemables_sorting_rule
          <br />
          <code>string</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>
          Defines redeemables sorting rule.
          <code>CATEGORY_HIERARCHY</code>
          means that redeemables are applied oaccording to the category priority.
          <code>REQUESTED_ORDER</code>
          means that redeemables are applied in the sequence provided in the request.
        </p>
        <p>
          Available values:
          <code>CATEGORY_HIERARCHY</code>,
          <code>REQUESTED_ORDER</code>
        </p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          redeemables_products_application_mode
          <br />
          <code>string</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>
          Defines redeemables products application mode.
          <code>STACK</code>
          means that multiple discounts can be applied to a product.
          <code>ONCE</code>
          means that only one discount can be applied to the same product.
        </p>
        <p>
          Available values:
          <code>STACK</code>,
          <code>ONCE</code>
        </p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          redeemables_no_effect_rule
          <br />
          <code>string</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>
          Defines redeemables no effect rule.
          <code>REDEEM_ANYWAY</code>
          means that the redeemable will be redeemed regardless of any restrictions or conditions in place.
          <code>SKIP</code>
          means that the redeemable will be processed only when an applicable effect is calculated.
        </p>
        <p>
          Available values:
          <code>REDEEM_ANYWAY</code>,
          <code>SKIP</code>
        </p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          no_effect_skip_categories
          <br />
          <code>array</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>
          Lists category IDs. Redeemables with a given category are skipped even if the
          <code>redeemables_no_effect_rule</code>
          is set to
          <code>REDEEM_ANYWAY</code>.
          Category IDs can't overlap with the IDs in
          <code>no_effect_redeem_anyway_categories</code>.
        </p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          no_effect_redeem_anyway_categories
          <br />
          <code>array</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>
          Lists category IDs. Redeemables with a given category are redeemed anyway even if the
          <code>redeemables_no_effect_rule</code>
          is set to
          <code>SKIP</code>.
          Category IDs can't overlap with the IDs in
          <code>no_effect_skip_categories</code>.
        </p>
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>
          redeemables_rollback_order_mode
          <br />
          <code>string</code>
        </p>
      </td>
      <td>
        <p>
          Defines the rollback mode for the order.
          <code>WITH_ORDER</code>
          is a default setting. The redemption is rolled back together with the data about the order, including related discount values.
          <code>WITHOUT_ORDER</code>
          allows rolling the redemption back without affecting order data, including the applied discount values.
        </p>
        <p>
          Available values:
          <code>WITH_ORDER</code>,
          <code>WITHOUT_ORDER</code>
        </p>
      </td>
    </tr>
  </tbody>
</table>
